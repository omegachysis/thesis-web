Here is a place to see ongoing questions or general ideas that have not been formalized well enough to become tasks on their own yet; also here is found questions that may not be sufficient for this thesis or may be too difficult to answer yet.

Open questions & Things to think about now:


Is it possible in a realistic timeframe to use these methods to evolve CA that maintain homeostasis in an environment of finite resources? Is it possible to even develop the concept of resource-gathering in a stochastic model?

With the stochastic mass-preserving models tried so far, it is clear that there can be some sense of 'separation' that occupies a finite volume, as expansion cannot go unlimited. However, it is not clear what it might mean to think of the environment as having 'energy' or resources that can be 'gathered' by an evolutionary CA in the model.

Asssuming Evo-CA with competitive resource-gathering behaviors can be generated, can we transition from genetic algorithms to selection based on those simulations alone?

While it makes sense to use genetic algorithms or some other evolutionary method on isolated simulations of each individual to initially form those individuals and analyze their complexity, it might make sense to eventually examine how those individuals interact or even try to observe emergence on the population level, not just on the invidual level. This opens the potentiality of analyzing co-evolution on top of basic self-organiziation, but it is not clear now how that might work or what the ideal time to switch would be, or if that is even a feasible step.

Should we focus on evolving initial states, cell rules, environmental factors, or all three in a hybrid model?

We can either fix stochastic rules and environments and evolve the starting state of the system to attempt to produce emergent behavior, do the same but evolve rules instead, or we can fix the other two and evolve the 'environment', or the way the cell values are changed dynamically throughout the simulation (e.g. we might inject random spikes in mass into the simulation as it runs to simulation inputs of 'energy'). Maybe generating emergent behavior the eventually mimics microbiological processes will be found the fastest by taking on a hybrid model of all of these factors. However, there might be some crossover between the fitness function and these adjustments in an undesireable way (e.g. random injections of energy might be indistinguishable from self-organization on some levels).

How complex does the model need to be to support features like self-replication evolving without fine-tuning?

We already know that fairly simple transition rules on simple binary CAs are enough to have Turing-completeness as well as self-replication (see: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Life-like_cellular_automaton), but no substantial evidence has been produced of evolving cellular automata with self-replication where the rules are not fine-tuned for producing said features. If the rules are stochastic, how complex do they have to be to produce self-replication and other life-like behavior? With completely local behavior, it is much harder to conclude the a minimal set of particles and/or physical laws is enough to simulate complex behavior. Real biochemical processes which do have a very limited set of base elementary particles and physical laws guide very complex formations but are governed by non-local forces and equations of motion that cellular automata do not simulate. A natural hypothesis might then be that the cells in a CA capable of life-like behavior are actually analogous to fields and groups of cells are analogous to particles instead of the usual model that cells in a CA are analogous to chemicals or even subsystems of an organism. If this is the case, even the most rudimentary life-like behavior might only evolve without fine-tuning under limited sets of cells or rules when the grid is absurdly large.

What real-life structures would the cells in an evolving-CA be analogous to if life-like behavior is observed? Particles? Chemicals? Proteins? Organelles? Organisms? Maybe none of these?
